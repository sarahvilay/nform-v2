An Integrated Intranet

Services Provided

Content Analysis
Strategic Planning
Information Architecture
Wireframes
Metadata Schema

Overview

When Servus Credit Union acquired several other credit unions, internal information was spread across four intranets containing overlapping and out-dated content. Employees were frustrated by how difficult it was to find colleagues with the right skills that were located at other locations. Migrating content from legacy intranets to a new consistent platform posed many challenges but Servus was ready to move forward.

We designed the core information architecture—the metadata system—connecting policies, procedures, banking systems, branch locations and other business details for staff to quickly find content. Site content was organized to support employees in member service functions.

Solution

We worked with Servus' in-house team and another vendor, Habanero. Our goal: balance the need of timely access to relevant information with organizational communications focus. This project involved inventorying nearly 16,000 pages of content spread across four intranets and planning their integration into one intranet.

Employees can now view content by topic, banking system and region to filter information application to a specific member. There are more than 200 distributed content creators and authors, and 40 employees approving content. Servus' intranet won Canada's Best Intranet at the 2012 SharePoint Summit.

Result

This project birthed a new survey method we call the content prioritization survey. We used the new method and other survey-based evaluation tools to assess staff intranet usage across branches and job functions to prioritize content.

Update: Matt B., Servus Manager of Digital Experience recently presented at our Better Intranets Conference and gave a few great tips about lessons learned. He suggested that taking the time to research your users helped engage the entire organization. Also, he strongly recommends using metadata over a folder structure anyday!
